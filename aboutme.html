Popular Pandey, Ph.D.

Complex biological processes occur in the nanoscale (1-100 nm) regime. ‘DNA’ which is just 2 nm in dimension is a fundamental building block of all life. ‘Hemoglobin’, a blood protein that transports oxygen throughout our body is only 5 nm in diameter. The structure, composition, and dynamics of these nanoscale entities determine their biological functions. A slight alteration in the structure and composition can lead to the malfunction of the protein which is key to various diseases including cancer. Therefore, the development of the facile, cost-effective, sensitive, and versatile the single-molecule biosensing approach is essential to characterize both the average properties as well as rare and dynamic changes of these nanoscale entities.

I am an interdisciplinary biophysics researcher interested in single-molecule detection and analysis, cellular biophysics, and Nano-biotechnology. Recently, my research focused on the development of a novel single-entity detection method by fabricating the nanopore and nanoelectrode integrated nanopipette for multi-mode electroanalytical detection of individual nanoparticles (NPs), biomolecules, and single cell. The nanopipette based multi-mode biosensing platform has great potential to be used as a smart sensor for various biomedical applications, health monitoring, quality control, and environmental sensing.
